## 🧱 Field summary

- `domain` — Fully qualified domain name.
- `status` — Current acquisition state for the domain in the public extract.
- `purchase_price` — Visible purchase price when available.
- `renewal_price` — Visible renewal price when available.
- `attractiveness` — Composite naming score used as a decision-support signal.
- `demand` — Relative buyer-pressure score when available.
- `length` — Character count without the TLD.
- `registrar` — Registrar name when known.
- `created_at` — Creation timestamp when known.
- `expires_at` — Expiry timestamp when known.

## ⚠️ Methodology and caveats

These domains are all one-word names on the .studio extension. That creates a clear pattern: words that can suit creative work, production, design, photography, media, or modern brand positioning. In this set, the median ask is 39.35, which makes price only one part of the decision. The stronger names are usually simple dictionary words with clear commercial meaning, easy spelling, and a natural fit with .studio, such as photo.studio or exhibit.studio. Be more careful with names that are unusually long, hard to say, or likely to raise trademark questions, such as QueenElizabeth.studio. When comparing these domains, weigh brand clarity, extension fit, renewal economics, and legal risk together.

- Prefer words that fit .studio naturally, like photo or exhibit
- Check renewal cost if a low ask hides a costly long-term hold
- Avoid names with obvious trademark or identity risk
- Favor clear spelling, strong recall, and commercial relevance
